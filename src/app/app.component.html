In 2019, we generated 56.3 million metric tons of electronic waste globally.

17.4% was properly recycled.
What happened to the rest?

High-income nations like the United States send most of their e-waste one of two places:

1. OVERSEAS

E-waste is often exported to countries like China, India, and Ghana for processing.

E-waste sent overseas is delivered to recycling plants where precious metals are extracted for resale.

Trade and recycling of e-waste overseas is often unregulated, leading to dangerous recycling practices.

Burning of electronic components during processing creates fumes that expose workers to dangerous heavy metals.

2. LANDFILL

The rest goes to the landfill. In the US, E-waste makes up 70% of hazardous waste in landfills.

Precious metals cannot be recovered for reuse from e-waste in landfills.

The precious materials in discarded electronics are limited resources that should be recovered and reused.

Heavy metals from e-waste in landfills can leach into groundwater, causing adverse health effects for surrounding communities.

Both of these options have consequences. The solution? Reduce your impact!

1. Hold onto your electronics! Reduce your footprint by using your devices for as long as possible before replacing them.

2. Pass it on! Consider giving unused devices to friends or family -- or, look for programs that donate old electronics. 1

3. Do your research! Find local e-waste drop-off events or facilities and ensure your electronics are recycled properly. 2
